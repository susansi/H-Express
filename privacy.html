Privacy Notice 

Last Update Status: Updated April 2022
 
The purpose of this policy is to define a Web application security assessment in H Express. 
Perform Web application evaluations to identify potential or recognized vulnerabilities due to unintentional 
configuration errors, weak authentication, inadequate error handling, sensitive information leakage, and so on. 
Finding and following up on these issues will limit the attack surfaces available to H Express services both internally 
and externally, and satisfy compliance with any relevant policies.
 
-We collect your personal information in order to provide and continually improve our products and services.
· Information You Give Us: We receive and store any information you provide in relation to H Express. 
  You can choose not to provide certain information, but then you might not be able to take advantage of many of 
  our H Express. For our current website, the data that is being collected include your email address and password.
  
-Threats:
To protect the protections of your password may be lost, we may send Verify code. 
Protections could be to use 2FA and a password manager.
-Impact:
If your data is lost or hacked, your account could be used by others. 
Information in your email could be hacked as well. Any personal information in the sign up process is unsecured. 
As for the cost analysis, we will use 2FA and password manager to protect these high-value passwords to avoid 
greater losses caused by the loss of user information.
 
-Startegy
All security issues that are discovered during assessments must be mitigated based upon the following risk levels. 
a) High – Any high risk issue must be fixed immediately or other mitigation strategies must be put in place to limit 
exposure before deployment. Applications with high risk issues are subject to being taken off-line or denied release 
into the live environment.
b) Medium – Medium risk issues should be reviewed to determine what is required to mitigate and scheduled accordingly. 
 Applications with medium risk issues may be taken off-line or denied release into the live environment based on the 
 number of issues and if multiple issues increase the risk to an unacceptable level. Issues should be fixed in a patch/
 point release unless other mitigation strategies will limit exposure.
c) Low – Issue should be reviewed to determine what is required to correct the issue and scheduled accordingly.
